Online crater scaling tools¶

Note that the first two online calculators assume a gravitational accelartion for the Moon and Earth, respectively. To first order, the transient crater size on other bodies is scaled by the ratio of the gravitational accelerations.

  • Moon only: Lunar Cratering Calculator -- an explanation of the equations behind the calculator
  • Earth only: Earth Impact Effects Program -- explanation of the equations
  • Any body: Crater scaling from impact parameters or from final crater size

WARNINGS! Things to keep in mind¶

These scaling laws are developed in a homogeneous, flat (planar) surface. They do not account for crust-mantle transition or curvature of a body and its gravity field. Thus they are not applicable for events between similar sized bodies. In these cases, the Catastrophic Disruption Scaling is more appropriate.

The outcomes of impacts are sensitive to the magnitude of the impact velocity. The absolute velocity has a relatively small effect on scaling the size of the transient crater. It has a larger effect on scaling the amount of melting and vaporization, because shock-induced melting and vaporization requires reaching specific peak pressures.

Melting and Vaporization during Impact Cratering¶

The energy in the shocked state is given by the conservation equation:

\(E-E_1 = (1/2)(P+P_1)(V_1-V)\)

The irreversible work is the energy in the shock wave minus the energy recovered during decompression. If the irreversible work is sufficiently great, the material may reach the melting point (or boiling point) after release to ambient pressures.

Key paper on estimating the amount of melting and vaporization during impact cratering: [PVM97] (newer calculations in [BC11] and [KSS11]).
